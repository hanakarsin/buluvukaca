University of Nairobi school of medicine: fees structure, programmes, requirements

The University of Nairobi school of medicine is one of the most prestigious schools of the famous Kenyan university. It is not surprising that it is one of the best training colleges in East Africa and beyond. The school focuses on imparting useful skills to allow students to graduate as competent medical practitioners. This is achieved through the different courses.

Nairobi University is one of the best universities in Kenya and East Africa. It is especially revered for its excellent programs in the different fields of study. One of the schools that make it famous is the school of medicine.

UON medicine entry requirements

There are basic requirements and qualifications that make it possible for potential candidates to pursue a medical course in UON. Understanding these requirements makes it easy for students to apply for their preferred medical courses and get in. The requirements vary depending on the specific course one intends to pursue.

However, there are a few UON school of medicine entry requirements that every student must meet. It does not matter the area of specialization one wants to pursue. These are the basic qualifications that offer students an opportunity to pursue a medical course at the university.

The qualification for joint admission board(JAB) students depends on whether students meet minimum university requirements. Besides that, they should meet enrolment requirements which depend on the KCSE cluster subjects for specific courses. These are as follows.

Alternative A

  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Physics/Mathematics
  • English/Kiswahili

Alternative B

  • Biological sciences
  • Physical sciences
  • Mathematics
  • English/Kiswahili

Besides JAB, there is the self-sponsored module II. The University Senate determines the admission to the Module II programmes through the school admissions committee. The admission for this category is based on the set minimum requirements for each program.

Basically, K.C.S.E. holders must have scored As in the regular program. It is also important to have an average of B- (minus) in the 4 cluster subjects discussed above, as long as no subject in the cluster shall have a score below C+ (plus). With these minimum grades required for medicine courses, students can apply knowingly to increase their chances of admission.

Medicine courses at UoN

The best university to study medicine in Kenya offers several courses at different levels. There are specific courses for both undergraduate and post-graduate levels. These courses vary in terms of duration and requirements. They include:

Undergraduate courses

Undergraduate courses have different requirements besides the basic grade requirements for general medical courses. They are also completed in different periods and have varied codes. They include:

  • Bachelor of medicine and bachelor of surgery: This takes 6 years to complete
  • Bachelor of Science in human anatomy: It takes a year to complete
  • Bachelor of Science in medical physiology: It takes 4 years to complete
  • Bachelor of Science in biochemistry: It takes 4 years to complete
  • Bachelor of Science in medical laboratory science and technology: Takes 4 years to complete

Masters programmes

Apart from understanding the Bachelor of Medicine and Surgery requirements at UON, it also helps to note that there are a few masters courses that interested students can pursue. Like the undergraduate courses, they also have varying durations. They include the following:

UON school of medicine fee structure

The fee structure for each course is released once a candidate qualifies for the course. It is in this structure that details about the fees amounts are outlined. These details vary from one medical course to the next and may change with time. Therefore, it's advisable to confirm every year with the university.
