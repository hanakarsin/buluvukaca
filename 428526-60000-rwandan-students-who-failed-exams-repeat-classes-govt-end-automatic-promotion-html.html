60,000 Rwandan Students Who Failed Exams to Repeat Classes as Govt End Automatic Promotion

  • Previously in Rwanda, students were consistently continuing to the next class regardless of their performance
  • The government said the move was aimed at improving the quality of education where learners will be promoted based on merit
  • On the other hand, in 2018, Kenya adopted a policy to ensure that all learners transit from primary to secondary levels of education

The Rwandan government has ended the automatic promotion of students to the next class.

As a result, more than 60,000 learners from the country who failed the national examinations will have to repeat their classes.

According to the results released by the country's Ministry of Education, more than 44,100 pupils who sat for their primary level exams and 16400 students in the ordinary level performed poorly.

The move is aimed at improving the quality of education where learners will be promoted based on merit.

Previously, students in the country were consistently continuing to the next class regardless of their performance.

100% transition

On the other hand, in 2018, Kenya adopted a policy to ensure that all learners transit from primary to secondary levels of education dubbed 100% transition.

The transition policy has since registered impressive progress so far, with a reported rate to secondary schools standing at 98% for the 2020 KCPE candidates.

In a previous address, Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha said poverty and parents' attitude have been a major hindrance to achieving 100% uptake.

For the 2020 candidates, the CS said the government managed to account for 100% of the 1,171,265 candidates who were selected to join Form One.

Of the candidates, Magoha said 1,129,637 had been placed in Form one in public and private schools, with some 2,658 having registered to re-sit the 2021 KCPE.

He added that some 11,213 have joined TVET while 1,193 either moved away from their homes or died.

From the data, Magoha said the transition policy had been a success.

In the policy's inaugural year, the government achieved 83.3% transition, while in 2019, it achieved slightly above 97% transition.

In a previous address, President Uhuru Kenyatta said he wants the 100% transition policy by his administration from primary to secondary school to be a success.

He urged everybody, including lawmakers to join in effecting the policy saying they should use Constituency Development Fund (CDF) to help needy students.
