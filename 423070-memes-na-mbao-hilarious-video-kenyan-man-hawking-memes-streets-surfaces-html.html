Memes Na Mbao: Hilarious Video of Kenyan Man Hawking Memes on Streets Surfaces

  • The man is seen in undated clip advertising memes, which he sells at KSh 20 each
  • On the banner on which he does the advertisement, he states that the memes are aimed at helping people fight depression
  • However, it yet to be known how he delivers the memes once a person buys, given the memes can easily be found online

People always say Kenya is some wonderland, and the assertion has always been made good by various happenings.

It can only be in Kenya where one can spot an opportunity to make ends meet in hawking memes.

A man identified as Crazy Jow showed up in one of the Nairobi streets selling memes at KSh 20.

In an undated viral clip making rounds on social media, Jow is seen wielding a banner onto which he advertises the memes in what he says is a bid to help Nairobians fight depression.

"Memeprenuer. Angalia Memes na Mbao KSh 20 (Have a look at the memes at only KSh 20). Stop depression," read the banner.

Man charges Nairobians for showing them direction

Elsewhere, another man tried to make a shilling out of showing the direction to those who are new in the Nairobi CBD.

In a hilarious clip that recently went viral, the funnyman was spotted advertising his services to show lost people directions in Nairobi town for only KSh 10.

The man, dressed in a long shirt, coat, tie and jeans, held a placard written, "Usipotee tao, uliza directions hapa. Ni 10 bob pekee (sic)."
