Former radio host Tina Kaggia risks losing her hand as she tries to remove wedding tattoo

- Tina tattooed a wedding band on her finger while marrying ex hubby JB Masanduku

- She went to a tattoo remover to get the ink off but the procedure did not go well

- The media personality was advised to seek help from a surgeon for reconstructive surgery or risk losing her wrist

When former Classic 105 radio presenter Tinah Kaggia and her ex-hubby JB Masanduku exchanged marital vows, they thought of the most unique way to seal their promise.

Unlike most people who go for real wedding rings, the two went for another option; tattooing her her finger with a wedding band.

However, the two parted ways years later leaving Tina with the burden of removing the wedding band tattoo ffrom her finger.

Well, the lass sought the services of a tattoo remover in Nairobi but the procedure did not go well and left her with a bad wrist.

She revealed she may have to do surgery after she suffered a botched ink removal.

Tina revealed in an interview with Pulse the blunder left her with less and riskier options such as reconstructive surgery to ensure she does not loose her hand.

''I paid for a consultation with their ‘dermatologist’, who informed me it would take about three sessions to have it completely erased. I did the first session, the ink started peeling off. The second session, there was no change,” she said.

The lass stated she will soon undergo the surgery to salvage her finger and promised to drag the tattoo removers to court for compensation.

"Eventually when they responded, I had seen a doctor at AAR who urged me to book an appointment with a surgeon for reconstructive surgery, or else I'd risk losing not just a finger, but an entire wrist,” she said.“All I wanted was compensation for what they did and the money I spent, but now I will take them to court,” she added.
