Nakuru Widower Pleads for Help to Find Daughter Who Has Been Missing for 15 Months

  • Bella Martin has been missing since April 12, 2020, when she walked out of her home but never returned
  • The 15-year-old had just returned home from school where she had just started as a form one student
  • According to her father, nobody has ever called him to demand a ransom

Since his daughter went missing over a year ago, Martin Shikuku is a restless father who has become accustomed to sleepless nights.

Bella Martin, his youngest child, has been missing since April 12, 2020, when she walked out of their compound and never returned.

The 15-year-old had just returned home from Kambala Girls' Secondary School, where she had just started as a form one student a few weeks before.

“My daughter went missing on Easter Sunday last year. She walked out of the compound at around noon and never came back," said Shikuku.

"We made a missing person report at Molo Police Station vide OB number 19/13/04/2020 and to date, no one knows where she is,” he added.

Shikuku had lost his wife in 2011 and was raising their three children on his own. Bella's absence, he stated, crushed his heart.

The recent disappearances of children bring back memories of his daughter, but he believes that his story will end happily.

According to him, no one has ever called him to demand a ransom.

“I have visited all places. I have reached out to family and friends. It would have been a little easier if anyone asked for ransom which I am willing to pay just to reunite with my daughter,” he said.

Bella, according to Shikuku, looks a lot like his late wife. Her older brother, Dismus Mukhwana, claimed his sister's departure had left a great void in the family.

Mukhwana further noted that Bella always consulted him whenever she needed assistance or advice.

"When we lost our mother in 2011, we had each other’s back to overcome the grief," said Mukhwana.

Every time I visit home, it reminds me of the pain of losing our mother and uncertainty over my sister,” he added.

Albert Mwale, one of Bella’s teachers, described her as an outgoing girl and a hardworking student.

The police and family are now calling on Kenyans to give any information to help trace her.
