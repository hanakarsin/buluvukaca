Reuben Abati Clash With APGA Chairman, Victor Oye On Live TV (Video)

Former Presidential spokesperson and TV anchor, Reuben Abati clashed with Dr. Victor Oye, the National Chairman of APGA, on National television.

Naija News reports that the clash started after Abati asked Mr. Oye to take full responsibility for the defamatory statements he made about another politician during the program.

Mr. Oye, however, told Abati to shut up, which infuriated Abati who immediately expressed his displeasure, arguing that Mr. Oye has no right to talk to him like that.

Oye denied telling Abati to shut up which led to an argument between the both of them.

It would be recalled a former ally to President Muhammadu Buhari and one of the founding fathers of All Progressive Congress (APC), Buba Galadima also verbally attacked a TV Host on Channels Television.

The politician who is a new member of the New Nigeria Peoples Party (NNPP) after decamping from the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) made a derogatory statement when asked if his party was capable of overthrowing the APC in the upcoming 2023 presidential election, the Cable reports.

Galadima maintained that no presidential aspirant across all the political parties can compare to the credibility of Rabiu Kwankwaso who is the presidential flag bearer of the NNPP.

According to him: “Senator Rabiu Musa Kwankwaso is the only politician in Nigeria that has got the CV that nobody has among all the flock that are running across the country.”

In a follow up question, Seun, the host of the show, asked if the NNPP has a structure in place that is strong enough to claim victory at the 2023 polls.

He responded: “Seun, I think you are losing your brain, somehow. You don’t know me. I only need three months to establish a party and win the presidential election, if there is a free, fair, and transparent election. That is my field.”
