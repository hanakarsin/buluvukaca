Adekunle Gold biography

The singer/songwriter/graphic designer was born Omoba Adekunle Kosoko on January 28th, 1987. He was born and raised in Ifako-Agege and Ikotun, Lagos State.

How old is Adekunle Gold?

As of 2021, the singer is 34 years old. He celebrates his birthdays on the 28th day of January each year.

Is Adekunle Gold from a royal family?

Yes, he actually is. Gold was born into the royal Kosoko family of Lagos State, Nigeria, and he bears the title of 'Prince'.

His parents are teachers, and he has three younger sisters. Gold is also the nephew of globally acclaimed actor Prince Jide Kosoko.

Another fun fact about his family is that although Gold's parents are Muslim, they allowed their children complete religious freedom.

Education

For his primary and secondary school education, Adekunle attended schools in Lagos State, Nigeria, from where he got his First School Leaving Certificate and West African Senior School Certificate.

He then joined Lagos State Polytechnic and graduated with a Higher National Diploma (HND) in Arts and Design.

Career

Gold began his singing career as a member of a local church's junior choir. As he has revealed in the past, he drew his musical inspiration from the likes of King Sunny Ade and Evangelist Ebenezer Obey.

At the age of 15, he wrote his first song and joined a band called The Bridge, which released a couple of singles before splitting.

In 2014, he released his debut single, Sade, a cover of One Direction's Story of My Life. The song, which was mixed by top Nigerian singer and songwriter Simi, went viral. The following year, the song earned him the Best Alternative Song award at the Headies.

YBNL Nation label CEO Olamide then signed Gold, who released two more Simi-mixed singles, Orente and Pick Up.

Adekunle released his debut album, titled Gold, on July 18, 2016. The album was released under the YBNL Nation and was produced by Pheelz, B Banks, Sleekamo, Oscar, Masterkraft and Seyikeyz. Gold charted number 7 on the Billboard World Album Chart for the week.

His sophomore album, About 30, featured guest spots by Dya on Down with You, Seun Kuti on Mr. Foolish, and Flavour N'bania on Yoyo. According to Gold, the 14-track album is a reflection of heart, fears, faith and pleasure.

Soon after the expiration of his contract with YBNL, Adekunle launched his band - The 79th Element (which refers to the atomic number of Gold in chemistry). The singer also owns the Afro Urban, a label he runs by himself with a couple of team members.

Adekunle Gold and Simi

Adekunle Gold is married to Nigerian singer Simi. Simi and Adekunle Gold wedding took place at a private ceremony in Lagos, in December 2019.
