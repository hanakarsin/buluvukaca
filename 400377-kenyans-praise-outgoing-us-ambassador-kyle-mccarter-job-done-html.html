Kenyans praise outgoing US ambassador Kyle McCarter for job well done

- Kyle McCarter was deployed to Kenya by the outgoing US President Donald Trump administration in 2019

- The Swahili-loving envoy said it had been a pleasure to serve his home country and Kenyans

- Kenyans praised him for a job well done and wished him well as he exits office

Kenyans have praised outgoing United States ambassador Kyle McCarter as he leaves office to pave way for a new appointee under the president-elect Joe Biden's administration.

McCarter was deployed to Kenya by the outgoing former US president Donald Trump's administration in 2019 replacing Robert Frank Godec who had held the position since 2012.

Taking to his Twitter to bid Kenyans farewell, the Swahili-loving envoy said it had been a pleasure to serve his home country and Kenyans.

"It has been my honour and pleasure to serve my country and the Kenyan people as ambassador. Time to move on. I will not say goodbye but we shall see each other again," he said.

Kenyans on Twitter flooded the comment section of his post to praise him for a job well done and wished him well as he leaves his plum job.

Glady's Mungai said:

"You served well. Onto the next assignment!"

Wairuma wrote:

"You did it well and kept the faith. Always loved your choice of Swahili, that's why you will always remain Rafiki wa Kenya, na nchi yako itakuwa #USAMarafiki kwa Kenya milele".

Sir Charles said:

"Go well. You will go down as the most accessible Balozi (envoy) in recent times! Your twitter moments will never be forgotten. Should you decide to embrace polygamy, pick a Kenyan".

George Owiso said:

"It was a wonderful experience working and collaborating with you. We cherish the moments we celebrated these partnerships with you and Victoria playing such wonderful host".

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, the US envoy announced on Friday, January 15, that he will be stepping down to pave the way for a new appointee who will serve under the administration of Biden.

During his tour of duty, McCarter has been a key point man of the US in Kenya and has not only been keen on cementing the diplomatic relation between Washington DC and Nairobi but has been instrumental in the fight against corruption.

He has been calling for accountability of donations from the US insisting that those who will be found culpable of pilfering such funds will be dealt with accordingly.
