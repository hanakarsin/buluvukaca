Understanding the stock market in Kenya

In Kenya, stock investments will never run out of style since big companies are always operational and present stock market opportunities. The Nairobi Stock Exchange was established in 1954 and currently, it continues to avail new products such as REITs, ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds), Carbon Credits, Financial and Commodity Derivatives as well as Mobile based products for example M-Akiba. The products traded at NSE are shares/equities and bonds/debt instruments.The NSE is located in the central business district of Nairobi, on the first floor of the Nation Center building. Trading takes place only five days a week Monday to Friday and between 10 am and noon.

How to invest in stock market in Kenya

When an individual buys stock, he/she is buying a piece of the company. On the other hand, when the company needs to raise money, the company will issues shares through an IPO (Initial Public Offering) where the price of the shares will be set based on the number of shares being issued and the estimated net worth of the company. As a result, the company keeps the money that is raised and continues to grow the business while the shares/stocks are traded on the stock market in Kenya.

Stock Market Analysis Kenya

After setting defined investment goals, an investor can arrange for a meeting with a licensed stockbroker often known as an intermediary of the NSE to receive investment advice and execute orders from the investor. The stockbroker will assist the investor by conducting a stock market analysis Kenya of the investment vehicles. The investor can then make well-informed decisions based on the risks involved, returns and the period taken to receive the profits. Just like other investments, stocks has its advantages and disadvantages, therefore; it is advisable to get advice from the stock experts before buying any shares.

History of the stock market in Kenya

The NSE began when Kenya was still a colony under the British in the early 1920s. Back then, the stock market was an informal marketplace for local shares and stocks. The stock market was not formal, therefore, it did not have rules and regulations to govern the trading activities. During this time, stock trading was a side business done by lawyers, accountants, real estate agents and auctioneers who met and exchanged prices based on a gentleman's agreement.

In 1951, Francis Drummond, an estate agent, established the first professional stock exchange firm. Francis approached Sir Ernest Vasey, the finance minister of Kenya back then who suggested the setting up a stock exchange market in East Africa. Later on, in July 1953, the two approached the London Stock Exchangeand the London officials accepted to recognize the setting up the NSE as an overseas stock exchange.

How to join the stock market in Kenya

Any potential investor has to open an account with a licensed stockbroker. Currently, there are over twenty brokers that trade on the stock market indices in kenya. It is advisable to go for the brokers that offer online trading platforms so that you can buy and sell shares at your convenience. The amount of these fee required to invest in the stock exchange market in Kenya varies depending on the broker. In most cases, the broker will need approximately Ksh 1200 to open an account and Kshs 100 monthly to keep the account active. In other cases, you will require Kshs 5000 if you want to invest in shares depending on the company you want to invest in.
