Busia Governor Sospeter Ojaamong' arrested

- Ojaamong is facing charges of conspiracy to steal from the county coffers

- The charges emanated from investigations by the Ethics and Anticorruption Commission

- The anti-graft body submitted his file on the investigation on June, 21, 2018

- After independent review of the file, DPP was satisfied there was sufficient evidence to prosecute Ojaamong'

- Eight other county officials are also facing arrest

Busia County governor Sospeter Ojaamong has been arrested a day after the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) Noordin Haji approved graft charges against him and eight other county officials.

Ojaamong' reportedly drove himself to the Ethics and Anticorruption Commission (EACC) Integrity Centre where he was held.

Reports reaching TUKO.co.ke on the morning of Wednesday, July 4, indicated the governor was being held by EACC sleuths at the integrity centre for interrogation.

The DPP on Tuesday, July 3, ordered for immediate arrest and prosecution of Ojaamong'.

Also to be arrested alongside the governor are other county officials who were accused of looting from the county government coffers.

In a statement dispatched to newsrooms, the DPP said Ojaamong' and the said county officials would face charges relating to irregular acquisition of integrated solid waste management services.

"It was alleged the governor of Busia, Sospeter Ojaamong', unilaterally signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Madam R Enterprises Limited (MRE Ltd) in a project of solid waste management system without due regard to the procedural framework governing management of public finance and projects leading to loss of KSh 8 million," the DPP noted.

The probe by the anti-graft body revealed there were two different entities using a similar name, Madam R Enterprises Ltd which was not registered at the time of the MoU and Madam R Enterprises which was registered as a business name in Kenya.

According to the DPP, Ojaamong' travelled to Germany where he signed an MoU with MRE Ltd obligating the Busia County government to pay KSh 8 million towards the waste management system.

"MRE Ltd was on the other hand obligated to source for and manage grants from well wishers to finance the project to the tune of KSh 8.9 billion," the DPP noted.

The governor allegedly approved the payment of KSh 8 million in two installments, the first installment of KSh 4 million was made to MRE and the second installment to MRE Ltd which by then was registered.

"MRE Ltd terminated the contract citing unfavorable political climate in Kenya and their inability to obtain international donor funds immediately after the second installment. The said project executed by MRE Ltd had not been provided for in the procurement plan for the financial year 2013/2014," the DPP said.

After conducting an independent review of the file from EACC, the DPP was satisfied there was sufficient evidence to warrant prosecution of Ojaamong' and the nine county officials implicated in the alleged theft of county funds.

The other suspects are Timon Otieno Mboya, Bernard Krade Yaite, Lenard Wanda Obimbira, Samuel Osieko Ombai, Allan Okwany Omachari, MRE Ltd, Edna Adhiambo Odongo, Renish Achieng' Omullo and Sabastian Hallensleben.

Ojaamong' however denied reports that he was arrested, insisting he voluntarily handed himself over to the authorities as a law abiding citizen.
