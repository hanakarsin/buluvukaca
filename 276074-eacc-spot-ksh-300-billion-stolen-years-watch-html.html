EACC on the spot as KSh 300 billion is stolen in six years under its watch

- The Senator said EACC has lost its uselfulness and needs to be disbanded

- He accused the commission's CEO for doing nothing as KSh 300 billion was lost under his tenure

- According to the legislator, Kenyans are pessimistic about EACC's effectiveness

- He accused the commission for focusing on minor corruption cases

- His National Assembly counterpart Serem informed the house Speaker he needed help to draft bill to disband EACC

Makueni Senator Mutula Kilonzo Junior has stated that Kenyans no longer need the Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) because it has terribly failed to tackle graft.

The legislator argued that the commission has nothing to offer Kenyans in battling corruption and accused the commission's CEO Halaqhe Waqo of sitting prettily as the country lost KSh 300 billion when he was in charge.

Speaking during a live broadcast monitored by TUKO.co.ke on Wednesday, June 5, when the EACC leadership faced Parliament's Justice committee, the Senator said Kenyans have become more pessimistic about the commission owing to its poor track record.

"It is hard to explain why Kenyans still need to have EACC when newspapers have more investigative articles and exposes about corruption. Halaqhe Wako, the six years you have been at the helm of EACC Kenya lost KSh 300 billion. What would you say is your scorecard?" posed Kilonzo.

Waqo had a difficult time responding to the Senator saying the commission had been sidelined in the current National Youth Service (NYS) scandal where over KSh 9 billion was allegedly swindled.

"We are sidelined in the second NYS case though in the first case in 2015 where KSh 791 million was involved we were the lead agency and prosecuted several suspects," he said.

Kilonzo could however not take any of Waqo's defense stating that even President Uhuru Kenyatta had publicly blasted him for failing him in the war against graft.

"The president is on record complaining you failed him by giving him a shallow list of suspects which never ended anywhere. Kenyans are pessimistic and have a reason to lose hope in you because the cases you have filed concern cases of as little as KSh 3,000 yet there were individuals who swindled millions of money," he said.

The EACC leadership was summoned in the legislative house to explain their inability to actively involve in current mega graft cases in the country.

TUKO.co.ke reported on Tuesday, June 6, reported that Aldai MP Cornely Serem had written to National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi seeking for help to draft a Bill to disband EACC due to its poor track record.

Serem argued EACC has nothing to show for over KSh 3 billion annual budget allocated to the commission and needed to be disbanded.
