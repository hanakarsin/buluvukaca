Mombasa robbery suspect allegedly clobbered to near death by police says life still in danger

A suspect who had earlier claimed police wanted to kill him says his life is still in grave danger at the Makupa Police Station where he was remanded.

Ali Ibrahim Aloice alias Mbala Mbala, who is facing charges of robbery with violence, raised the new concerns while appearing before Mombasa Senior Principal Magistrate Henry Nyakweba on Thursday, March 7.

TUKO.co.ke understands he made the claims in the presence of Mombasa’s Urban OCPD, Eliud Monari, who had been summoned by the court.

The OCPD was in court to answer earlier claims made on Tuesday, March 5, by the same suspect who alleged police officers at Makupa Police Station assaulted him and wanted to kill him.

The accused pleaded with the court not to take him back to the station and instead remand him at another police station claiming officers at Makupa were threatening him.

“Since I complained here on Tuesday on how they assaulted me the officers have been threatening to kill me saying I made the biggest mistake in my life. If I go back to that police station God knows whether I will come back in court alive or not,” the suspect told the court.

The OCPD denied the allegations by the suspect and even claimed it was the police who rescued the accused from an angry mob.

The senior officer further stated the accused was lying to the court because he was arrested by civilians who threatened to lynch him after he was caught in the act of stealing before the police arrived.

“When the police rescued the suspect the OCS Makupa Police station ordered the officers to take him to the hospital, and he was treated before being presented in court the following day,” said Monari.

However, the suspect said the police did not follow the court’s orders and did not take him to the hospital as claimed by the OCPD, and that instead they took him to the station where they allegedly threatened to kill him.

The suspect further alleged the officers hit him with bricks while he was still handcuffed and even forced him to jump into a fire when they arrested him.

The principal magistrate, Nyakweba, ordered the OCPD to investigate the allegations.

“If the suspect dies in custody because of the threats he is receiving, if true, his blood will be in my hands and I don’t want to be guilty of anything. We need a report from you as the OCPD concerning the allegations,” said Nyakweba.

The court granted the suspect's request to be transferred from Makupa Police Station by ordering that he be transferred to Central Police Station pending mentioning of the case.

The OCPD is expected to present a report on his findings upon completion of the ordered investigations on March 13, 2019.
