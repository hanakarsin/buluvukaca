Masters degree holder resorts to selling vegetables in Uasin Gishu county

- Susan Kanda, a master’s degree holder in procurement has been jobless for the last seven years

- The graduate runs a groceries business in Chepkoilel, Uasin Gishu county

- The University of Eldoret alumna has applied for over 20 jobs but all her efforts were futile

A master’s degree graduate holder from the University of Eldoret has resorted to selling vegetables to cater for her day to day needs after failing to find a job.

Susan Kanda who has a master’s degree in procurement and a bachelor’s degree in business management runs a vegetabe grocery business in Chepkoilel, Uasin Gishu county.

“Despite having a master’s degree in procurement, I have been jobless for the last seven years and my neigbours are questioning the need for education if one cannot find work to do,” Kanda said. “I have sent over 20 job applications to various offices countrywide including Nairobi but I have been unsuccessful even though I met all the job requirements,” she explained.

According to a KTN News video seen by TUKO.co.ke, Susan Kanda was helped by her neighbor set up a grocery business to make ends meet.

A new report dubbed Kenya Social Protection and Job Programmes Public Expenditure Review by the World Bank states that Kenya’s job creation has failed to keep pace with new job seekers in the market.

The report projects that unemployment levels in Kenya will rise to 10.5% from 9.3% in 2018.
