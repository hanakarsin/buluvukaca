Schurli: Giant Tortoise Who Lived Through the 1900s Dies Aged 130 at Zoo

  • A 130-year-old tortoise died at a zoo in Austria after being unwell for a few days
  • Schurli was the oldest animal at the zoo and lived with two other tortoises, Menschik and Madi
  • The giant reptile outlasted several zookeepers and loved to feed on cucumbers and dry leaves

A zoo in Vienne, Austria, is in mourning after the death of their oldest resident, a tortoise named Schurli.

The Tiergarten Schönbrunn Zoo was where Schurli called home for the last 68 years, after arriving in Austria from Seychelles back in 1953.

According to WFMY2 News, Schurli died on Sunday, July 11, after being unwell for a few days and was under close observation by veterinarians.

The tortoise reportedly outlasted zookeeper after zookeeper at the home and was known by many visitors.

Speaking in an interview, zookeeper Maximilian Schon said Schurli had a strong personality and was both adorable but stubborn.

The giant reptile was nicknamed "old guy" by zoo staffers and loved to feed on cucumbers and dry leaves.

Schurli lived in a terrarium enclosure with other two tortoises, a male called Menschik and a female named Madi.

According to WWMT News, the tortoise gained some popularity making weekly predictions at the 2016 European Championship and doing a great job.

Menschik is now the oldest resident at the zoo and arrived shortly after Schurli. An Aldabra giant tortoise can go up to 150 years.

Sam, who had ruled the park for over eight years, was found lying helpless on the morning of Monday, August 3, 2020.

According to the Wildlife At Risk International-Africa, the king of the jungle was found in a weak condition, with many infected bite wounds on the back and a major wound on his face.

He was unable to move, and when veterinary officers moved him to their facility for attending animals, he died while receiving treatment.

Sam died at the age of 15, barely eight months after his brother Cheru disappeared in December 2019.
