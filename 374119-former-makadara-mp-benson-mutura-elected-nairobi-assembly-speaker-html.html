Former Makadara MP Benson Mutura elected new Nairobi assembly Speaker

- At least 99 MCAs out of 122 voted in support of Benson Mutura to replace the former Speaker Beatrice Elachi

- The incoming speaker was sworn in shortly after the exercise and promised to discharge his duties in accordance with the law

- The low-key exercise had been conducted via video link in line with government COVID-19 guidelines barring physical gatherings

Nairobi county assembly has found a new Speaker barely a week after embattled Beatrice Elachi resigned from the hot seat bringing to an end incessant fights with the MCAs.

During a virtual election conducted on Friday, August 14, majority ward representatives voted to elect former Makadara MP Benson Mutura as Elachi's replacement.

Mutura garnered a total 99 votes in a seemingly rushed exercise that lacked pomp and colour which characterised past similar speaker elections inside the assembly.

Acting Speaker John Kamangu declared Mutura the winner after beating five other contenders who included ex-city deputy speaker Ken Ngondi and former Nairobi chief economic and planning officer Washington Makodingo.

"I hereby declare Mr Benson Mutura the new Nairobi county assembly Speaker, having attained more than the required two-thirds majority votes,"he announced.

Others were youthful politician and first runner-up in the 2017 Speaker election Abdi Ali Abdi, Mike Obonyo Guoro and Allan Chacha Mang’era.

The incoming speaker was sworn in shortly after the exercise, he promised to discharge his duties in accordance with the law irrespective of political affiliations.

Jubilee Party which has the majority MCAs held a consultative meeting with the city representatives a day before the election exercise where his candidature was endorsed.

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) resolved to back Mutura's candidature in exchange of the deputy speaker's seat in the spirit of the Building Bridges Initiative and the handshake.

"The truth is we have sat down and agreed to support Mutura. As a party we agreed to speak in one voice in order to support him," disclosed Nairobi ODM chairperson George Aladwa.

Elachi tendered her resignation letter on Tuesday, August 11, morning, citing frustrations and threats to her life as some of the reasons for stepping down.

It, however, emerged that she was actually pushed out over chaos and wrangles that threatened to bring the county on its knees.

Her fate was sealed at State House after President Uhuru Kenyatta asked her to step down over irreconcilable differences with MCAs.

The meeting that was held on July 31 was attended by 41 Jubilee Party ward representatives led by reinstated Majority Leader Abdi Guyo.

The MCAs who were tired of the constant fighting between embattled Governor Mike Sonko and Elachi raised their concerns with the president.

"We spoke with the president and he listened to us and acted accordingly. There are claims that Elachi did not follow due process in tendering her resignation, but we are going to impeach her even if she has resigned, so that we can seal off any loophole of her ever coming back. This time she will not catch us by surprise,” Roysambu MCA Peter Warutere said.
