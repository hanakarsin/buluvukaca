Over 1 million Kenyans file tax returns as deadline looms

- Kenyans with KRA PIN are expected to file their income tax returns by June 30, 2018

- Those who fail to file their tax returns by the set deadline risk fine of KSh 20,000

- The taxman said there was already a significant improvement in compliance

Have you filed your income tax returns for year 2017?

The rush to beat the June 30 deadline is on and more than a million Kenyans have already cleared with the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA).

TUKO.co.ke can reliably confirm some 1.3 million employees and businesses had filed their annual income tax returns as of Sunday, June 10.

According to the taxman, this was a significant improvement compared to the income tax returns filed between January and April 2017.

The returns filing for year 2017 commenced on January 1, 2018.

In attempts to give Kenyans enough time to file their returns, KRA decided to extend its official working hours.

It now opens its offices from 7.30 am and closes at 6 pm, a shift from the normal 8 am to 5 pm working hours.

The Authority also increased the number of its service centres so that more Kenyans can easily file their returns and get help where necessary.

You can now access KRA services at more than 50 huduma centres, in addition to 38 other service centres spread across the country.

The increase in number of working hours and service centres could have been informed by the extremely long queues witnessed in 2017, especially in the last days to the deadline.

The taxman said 2.4 million employees and businesses were able to file their returns by June 2017.

READ ALSO: New KRA PAYE Rates 2017

Reports seen by TUKO.co.ke indicate KRA was able to collect kSh1.3 trillion in 2017, which was below the National Treasury's target of KSh 1.4 trillion.

The 2018 target was set at KSh 1.7 trillion.

Every working Kenyan citizen is expected to file annual income tax return by the deadline, failure to which he or she risks being slapped with a fine of KSh 20,000.

The fine was revised upwards from KSh 1000 in 2015, a move that seems to have worked well for the taxman so far.
