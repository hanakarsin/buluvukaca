400 people die from road accidents in Nairobi every year - NTSA reveals

- Most road carnage incidents involve youth between 24 and 34 years

- NTSA stated most of the accidents are caused by human errors like drink driving

- Kenya recorded 2,919 road accident deaths in 2017 and NTSA wants to reduce the figure by 50% in the next two years

- 70% of road accident deaths involve pedestrians

At least 400 people die from road accidents in Nairobi every year, making it the county with the highest number of fatalities in Kenya.

In a statement seen by TUKO.co.ke, on Wednesday, May 17, National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) stated most of the accidents which happen in the city involve young people aged between 24 and 34 years.

Duncan Kibogong, NTSA safety coordination deputy director who addressed stakeholders at a safety workshop at Strathmore Business School said 400 of the 2,919 people who died from road accidents in Kenya in 2017 were from Nairobi.

Kibogong expressed concern that pedestrians were the highest fatalities of road carnage and called for strict adherence to road safety rules by the public.

"Pedestrians alone accounted for 70% of all the road accident deaths which is a a great concern. Other great victims were motorcycle riders and extra passengers," said Kibogong.

According to the NTSA official Thika ,Mombasa, and Jogoo roads as well as Airport North Road, Eastern Bypass, Lang’ata road and Waiyaki Way recorded high number of pedestrian deaths.

The NTSA statistics for the year 2017 released on Monday, January 8, 2018 showed road accident deaths reduced with a very small margin from 2,965 to 2,919.

"The number of deaths is still very high and we need to work together to reduce it because it is costing even the economy. The country loses about 5% of the national Gross Domestic Product (GDP) which is about KSh 350 billion annually," explained Kibogong.

The authority stated 80% of all the road crashes were occasioned by human errors and called for patience and caution from all road users.

"We cannot blame poor road infrastructure and the designs for the massive killings in the highways. Drink driving, hawking on roadsides, careless driving and crossing in unclassified roads is are the greatest causes of road carnage," Kibogong added.

The workshop delved in establishing how practical research would be useful in achieving the reduction of road carnage in Kenya by 50% by 2020.
